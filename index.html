Direct jet feedback via stellar entrainment

I calculated the power available for direct feedback into the ISMs of local radio AGN hosts due to deceleration of AGN jets from stellar winds. AGN jets must be decelerated in order to deposit kinetic energy into their host galaxies. I investigate the process of stellar mass-loading as a source of deceleration, where stellar winds are swept up by the jet. We calculate the impact of stellar mass-loading on the local jet population, to quantify the energy available to the ISMs of radio AGN hosts through direct feedback.

Shooting for the stars: Jet-mode feedback and AGN jet deceleration from stellar mass-loading, O'Shea T.M. et al.. Accepted for publication by ApJ.

Image Credit: Chandra X-Ray Observatory

Dynamical friction in galaxy clusters

I tested the effect of dynamical friction on measurements of the splashback radius in galaxy clusters. There are discrepancies between splashback radii (galaxy cluster sizes) measured with dark matter and galaxy number density profiles, which are often attributed to dynamical friction. I ran simulations starting from galaxy cluster data from cosmological simulation IllustrisTNG, and found that dynamical friction minimally affects the measured splashback radius, meaning that there must be other contributing processes that are not yet understood!

Dynamical friction and measurements of the splashback radius in galaxy clusters, O'Shea T.M. et al.. Accepted for publication by OJAp.

Mentorship and outreach efforts

I am passionate about promoting evidence-based mentorship practices in our field. I have participated in a number of mentoring programs and workshops, and am currently developing a research mentorship program at UW-Madison. Please reach out to talk more about mentorship strategies!

Peer mentorship

I run the peer mentorship program for first year graduate students at UW-Madison, alongside Jodie Kiyokawa. We pair older graduate students and incoming first years to support the transition to graduate school.
